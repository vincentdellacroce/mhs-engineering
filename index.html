Process

We start by
listening to the land.

Every site has a water story, a sun story, a soil story. We spend the first week doing nothing but measuring, observing, and asking questions. The design follows from what we find — not from a template.

Material selection
is half the work.

We source regionally — basalt from the Columbia Gorge, reclaimed brick from Portland demolition sites, Douglas fir from salvage mills. The material should belong to the place before it arrives.

We design for
the third year.

Newly installed landscapes are always awkward. We choose plants that have somewhere to go — that will fill, soften, and surprise you. Our maintenance agreements let us keep nudging the design as it matures.

§ About

Fourteen years of difficult sites.

Stoneform was started in 2011 by Cara Lindqvist after a decade working under landscape architects in Vancouver and Seattle. We're a crew of seven — two designers, four builders, one person who only does plants. We work on roughly twenty projects a year, all within the Portland metro area and the Gorge.

We don't pitch to commercial developers. Most of our work comes through the owners of old houses with bad drainage and big ambitions. That's where we do our best work.*

*selectively
