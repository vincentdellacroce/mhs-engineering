Selected Work

How We Work

I.

The site speaks first

Before drawings, before plants, before anything is committed to paper — we spend time on the land. Sometimes weeks. Soil conditions, drainage patterns, the angle of the afternoon sun in February. Nothing compensates for this step, so we do not skip it.

II.

Plans drawn by hand

Every design begins on paper. Our principal, Margaret Holt, has produced hand-drawn garden plans for fifteen years. The act of drawing by hand forces decisions that software defers. Each line is intentional.

III.

Installation
without haste

We do not subcontract installation. Our team plants everything we design. The schedule is set by the plants — by the seasons — not by a contractor's calendar. A garden installed correctly in eighteen months outlasts one installed incorrectly in six weeks.

About

A practice built on
restraint

Margaret Holt founded Grove Order after a decade assisting on private estate restorations in Charleston and Savannah. She has a particular reputation for formal parterre work — the precise geometry of clipped boxwood, the measured interval between standard roses — executed in the difficult heat and humidity of the Georgia coast.

Grove Order takes no unsolicited inquiries. Every client has come through a personal recommendation. We prefer it that way. The relationships are longer. The work is better.
